Celebrating Diwali in India

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant festivals in India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Diwali Celebrations

  • Clip of Diwali celebration: Short videos capturing the grand fireworks, beautiful diyas, and festive joy.
  • Photos of Diwali celebration: Stunning images of homes decorated with rangoli, candles, and lights.
  • Why it is celebrated: Diwali marks Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana and also honors Goddess Lakshmi.
  • When it is celebrated: Diwali falls on the new moon night of the Hindu month of Kartika, usually in October or November.
  • What you do on this day: People clean their homes, decorate with lights, exchange sweets, perform pujas, and burst fireworks.
